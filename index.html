Tactile Behavior Priors for Dexterous Reorientation

TacBPM distills multi-scale sphere specialists into a reusable tactile-proprioceptive latent controller, then freezes the tactile prior so downstream policies learn compact residual latent actions instead of raw joint commands while finetuning the decoder for new geometry.

Overview timeline: 0-2.5 s TacBPM motivation, 2.5-6.5 s multi-scale tactile prior, 6.5-13.5 s residual latent downstream reuse, 13.5-17.5 s multi-object rotation, 17.5-25.5 s continuous axis control in simulation, 25.5-39.5 s continuous axis control on the real robot, 39.5-45.5 s single-axis multiface comparisons, 45.5-50.5 s challenging single-axis comparisons, 50.5-53 s takeaway.

TL;DR

TacBPM learns a contact-aware behavior prior model for dexterous reorientation. Instead of training every new policy from low-level 22-DoF hand actions, TacBPM first trains sphere reorientation specialists across scale factors 0.3 to 1.0, distills them into a single variational latent controller, and reuses the frozen tactile-conditioned prior for downstream reorientation on harder objects.

The downstream policy predicts only a residual latent code around the prior mean. This keeps exploration close to stable contact behaviors while still allowing task-specific adaptation to anisotropic objects, multi-object settings, and commanded rotation axes.

Stage 1 Specialist behavior coverage

Eight sphere-scale teachers expose the hand to different apertures, tactile force patterns, and rolling or regrasping regimes.

Stage 2 Tactile latent controller

A variational encoder, learned prior, and decoder are conditioned on joint history, previous targets, contact magnitudes, 3D contact locations, and scale cues.

Stage 3 Residual latent transfer

Downstream policies keep the tactile prior fixed, learn compact residual latent actions, and finetune the decoder for geometry adaptation.

Method Overview

TacBPM treats tactile sensing as part of the hand action interface rather than as an auxiliary observation. The prior predicts a latent action distribution from the current tactile-proprioceptive interaction state; the decoder maps the selected latent code back to hand commands. During downstream learning, the policy outputs a residual latent action added to the prior mean, so it can adapt intent without abandoning the distilled contact behavior.

Teacher family 8 sphere scales

Scale factors 0.3 to 1.0 provide clean, controllable contact behavior coverage.

State 3-frame tactile history

Joint state, previous action targets, contact magnitudes, and 3D contact positions are concatenated into the behavior model.

Latent action 16-D residual code

The downstream policy controls the hand through a compact latent correction around the learned prior.

Reuse Frozen prior interface

The same tactile prior anchors downstream object reorientation, multi-object settings, and axis-conditioned rotation while the decoder adapts to downstream geometry.

Failure Cases

Representative failures show how real contact errors accumulate into drops, stuck grasps, slow rotations, out-of-distribution hand states, and command-switching failures.

Failure summary. Across the recorded No tactile and From scratch trials, failures mostly appear as drops after contact drift, stuck configurations, slow or side-biased rotations, and out-of-distribution hand postures. A rare TacBPM failure occurs under frequent command switching when an axis change arrives during unstable contact.
